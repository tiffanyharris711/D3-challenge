Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

There is an upward trend of percentage in poverty as the percentage without healthcare increases. The state of Texas has the highest rate without healthcare, while Mississippi has the highest percentage in poverty.

The data seems to cluster between the ages of 36 to 40 for those without healthcare. Utah has the youngest median age while Maine has the highest. Massachusetts has the least rate of those without healthcare.

The median household income is distributed between $40,000 to $75,000 for those without healthcare. There appears to be somewhat of a data cluster between $45,000 to $50,000. Mississippi has the lowest median income without healthcare while Maryland has the highest.
